Phase Portraits of Complex Functions

This tool visualizes complex-valued functions by assigning a color (HSB or HSV scheme) to each point in the complex plane according to its argument/phase. The identity function f(z)=z shows how colors are assigned.

f(z): Enter any expression in z. Here are some example functions to try

  • z^2
  • z^3-1
  • 1/z^5
  • (z+1)/(z-1)
  • sin(z)
  • e^z
  • log(z)
  • sech(z)
  • arctan(z)
  • (z-1)(conj(z)^2+conj(z)+1)
  • 0.926(z+7.3857e-2 z^5+4.5458e-3 z^9)
  • Jacobi Elliptic: sn(z, 0.3)
  • Gamma function: gamma(z)
  • Taylor Series: sum((-1)^n*z^(2n)/(2n)!, 7)
  • Atomic Singular Inner Function: prod(e^((z+(e^(2*pi*i/5))^n )/(z-(e^(2*pi*i/5))^n)), 5)
  • Iterated function: iter(z+z'^2,z,15)

Level curves: Draw level curves. Select an option 'Phase', 'Modulus', 'Phase/Modulus' or 'None'.

Zoom In/Out Drag or enter a positive number to zoom (in/out). You can also use the mouse wheel.

Parameters: You can use three parameters in your function, e. g. z + t, exp(u * z), z^n. Use sliders to change parameters, where t$\in$[0,1] and u = exp(i*s) with s$\in$[0, 2\pi]. Finally, an integer n$\in$[0,30].

Save (png): Click to save your plot.
